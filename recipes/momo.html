Veg. MoMo

Description

Savor the joy of Nepali momo, each bite a taste of home. Wholesome comfort, wrapped in memories, shared with love.

Ingredients

For the Dough

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • Water (as needed)

For the Filling

  • 2 cups finely chopped vegetables (cabbage, carrots, bell peppers)
  • 1 cup finely chopped onions
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch ginger, grated
  • 1 cup tofu or paneer, crumbled (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon chili sauce (adjust to taste)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Dipping Sauce

  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon finely chopped green onions
  • 1 teaspoon finely chopped cilantro
  • 1/2 teaspoon finely chopped garlic
  • 1/2 teaspoon finely chopped ginger

Instructions

For the Dough

  1. In a large bowl, mix the all-purpose flour and salt. Gradually add water and knead to form a smooth, elastic dough. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for at least 30 minutes.

For the Filling

  1. Heat 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil in a pan over medium heat. Add garlic and ginger, saut√© for a minute until fragrant.
  2. Add chopped onions and cook until translucent.
  3. Add the chopped vegetables and cook until they are tender but still have a crunch.
  4. Stir in crumbled tofu or paneer (if using) and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes.
  5. Add soy sauce, sesame oil, chili sauce, salt, and pepper. Mix well and cook for another 2 minutes. Remove from heat and let the filling cool.

Assemblng the Momo

  1. Divide the dough into small portions and roll each portion into a thin, round disc (about 3 inches in diameter).
  2. Place a spoonful of the vegetable filling in the center of the disc.
  3. Fold the edges of the disc to form a half-moon shape, pinching the edges to seal.
  4. Pleat the edges, if desired, for a traditional momo appearance.

Steaming the MoMo

  1. Grease a steamer basket or tray with oil to prevent momos from sticking.
  2. Place the momos in the steamer, making sure they are not touching each other.
  3. Steam the momos for 15-20 minutes or until the outer layer becomes translucent.

For the Dipping Sauce

  1. Mix soy sauce, vinegar, sesame oil, chopped green onions, cilantro, garlic, and ginger in a bowl.
  2. Stir it until everything is mixed together
Serve the momos hot with the dipping sauce on the side. Enjoy your homemade Nepali momos!
